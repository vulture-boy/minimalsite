Tyson Moll - Church into Condos

CHURCH INTO CONDOS, 2017

Remains of a church-to-be-gentrified.

Photos taken from an active construction site, after hours. I made my way inside believing the workers had left for the day but in reality they had just wrapped up their shift. Hearing a commotion, I hid behind a column of surplus pink styrine insulation and heard the gate lock as they left for home. This startled me, but assured that I could explore the site more freely after a pause. I ascended the wooden stairs and walked across flooring both new and of historic brick, taking photographs throuh frames and out upon the neighbourhood through openings. There were three instances of remaining stained glass, simple red and clear smeared with lettering. At the top, the massive iron truss suspended over the refinished roof, "Victims of Captialism" in red text aside the chimney. It felt like a cage bearing down on the church's remains, still bearing evidence of a tragic fire. Shallow pools collected strange debris, ranging from construction scraps to glossy blue party hats. I tried to capture the darkness of the interior, the enormity of the exposed truss frame, and a few otherworldly long exposures by bracing myself against the walls. Then I fled. Somehow this all comes back to me clearly with these records, seven years since. In case you wish to visit the building, you can find its completed form just north of Bloor and Dufferin.
