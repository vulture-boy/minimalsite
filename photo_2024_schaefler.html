Tyson Moll - Schaefler Ridge Hike

Schaefler Ridge Hike

Hiking through the Schaefler Ridge of the Appenzell Alps.

My first proper mountain hike! The Shaefler Ridge trail follows a series of peaks formed by colliding techtonic plates connecting the Ebenalp summit to the rest of Appenzell. You can read more about it here. At this time in June, the cows roam the hillsides, their bells echoing across all parts of the trail. The ascent was steep and I needed to take many pauses to catch my breath, making for great opportunities to take sweeping panoramas. There were some lovely cabins along the way; I had a delicious rosti and some mint tea at a lodge near the halfway marker. Were it not for my partner's keen research into backpacking and the gear she let me borrow it likely would have been a much more harrowing experience for me. I was likely the last person hiking along the 17+ km route I had chosen for the day; by the time I reached the cable cars it was well past closing time. I somehow made it down the mountain in 2hs in time to catch the last bus out from the summit without sustaining any injuries. Overall, I feel very fortunate for the experience and the circumstances aligning in my favour.
