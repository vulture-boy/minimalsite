Tyson Moll - Fisher Body Plant 2016

FISHER BODY 21, 2016

A massive derelict auto plant in Detroit's Midtown.

Fisher Body 21 is a massive abandoned auto plant in Detroit. Just north of downtown, the plant has sat abandoned since 1993 and features collapsed concrete flooring, spatterings of debris and remnants of the manufacturing process. The photos in this album not only contain images of the plant but also two neighbouring abandoned industrial lots, both of which have since been flattened into parking lots. There are also upcoming plans to begin development on the plant itself, presented publicly in 2023. In 2016, I rented a bicycle and visited the distrct. I first stopped by three story ruins to the west of the building, then entered another abandoned property north of the Fisher Body on Hastings St. I was regretably ill-prepared and did not bring a spare charge of batteries, so by the time I reached the infamous car manufacturing plant I was milking what lingering charge I could. The building was nothing short of a graffiti gallery, with at least a hundred artworks plastered on its surfaces and windows. Moss and vegetation took root in the dark corners and the roof's surface. I vowed to return within the next two years to better document the place; the experience of exploring the space was unforgetable and Detroit was rapidly changing for the better of its local population. As I was leaving the premises, locals from the state had come to record a video of their friend scootering up the concave surface formed from the second floor, which now rested upon the first.
