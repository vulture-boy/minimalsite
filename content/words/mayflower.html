Tyson Moll - Midnight Mayflower
Midnight Mayflower

tablespoons of melting butter, bubbling over the sides upon bowls of popcorn
i dress my evening's savoury 'dessert' in front of an 1920's art deco liquor cabinet. emptied of stock, its mirrored panelled display make iconic of a layman's meal.
the opening sequence of a late 90's anime splashes the CRT screen above the refridgerator. fireworks outside light in red, white and blue that, somehow, resemble popsicle treats moving at a monolithicly slow pace

hazy memories merging as not-memories, and the strange dreams of a child processing nightmares through the tribulations of their parents divorce. I lay in bed as I scribe my stream of conscious and dwell upon the meaning, if there is one. More, surprised to find one. Late nights past midnight, ceiling-gazing to wistful bedroom dreampop -- the cauldron for twilight dreams in some sort of liminal limbo space even Chubby Checker can't circumnavigate, up from down.

she's not home tonight. she'll be back, but for now its alone with my thoughts and the trials of self-regulation. Its surprisingly peaceful on the moon sometimes, feeling a little lighter in the air but not quite grounded. Or maybe I'm floating beneath a clear night sky by a beach, yearning to ride some inflatable crawdad like the ones we used to joust with in the summer pool as kids. I'm just in my bedroom, but as I progressively grow tired it seems more like the liminal space between dreamy destinations the brochures don't even tell you about.

When I'm unconscious, does my unawareness move between these dream spaces, 'physically' present but consciously dormant? Not to confuse metaphors, but when we sleep on the train across a country, we still visit the places we pass through, the circulated air still drawn into our lungs and becoming part of us. If a child scrawled a masque over my face while I dreamt, I'd have a tangible souvenir. So then, are these dream phenomenon brushing across my mind as I sleep leaving their own masque? The tears I draw from a nightmare are very much on my face when I escape the terrors.

I pause, take a breath. Another metaphysical pondering. I need to rest, put down the phone. I have real friends to look forward to, a vibrant neighbourhood to stroll, financial obligations to manage. Somehow the air feels fresher in the room, the worlds cast upon the stone plate. Intellectuals talk about dreams as a pondering mechanism of the brain to help solve its dilemmas. I feel thats what journaling is in this context. And now I begin to doubt that sleep and dreams are as 'unconscious' as we might believe them to be.

Good night. Sweet dreams. May we be at peace with our possibilities and live our best reality. May our Mayflower reach safe harbour and embrace unity with its shores.
