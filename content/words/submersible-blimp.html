Tyson Moll - submersible blimp
submersible blimp

my 'class' boarded a blimp that was parked in the harbor.
the professor joined me in my cabin and we looked out the window at chicago during a cloudy day.
the blimp then started to drive down the road and onto the highway, continuting to accelerate.
we passed by some large black and white panelled structures with antennae that rose diagonally towards the water, then took a left and started driving up a steep hill.
I was in the cockpit now (how?) and apparently my dad was driving and I asked him "Where did you learn how to drive a blimp??" and he just kept muttering under his breath "Yasha... Yasha...".
Anyways, the blimp apparently took off from the precipice of this steep hill between skyscrapers, wobbled for a moment and then was successfully in flight in the air.
I think I had it in my mind that this was a submarine instead of a blimp so I was thoroughly confused until I figured out blimps were in fact airborne flight vehicles...
I was also thoroughly distracted by all the things I wanted to photograph.
It then occurred to me that you don't drive a damn blimp down a runway with that air resistance, and that broke the immersion so much that I woke up.
