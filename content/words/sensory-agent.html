Tyson Moll - sensory agent
sensory agent

--Side A : synthetic--

memories are built with two-tined forks,
whether it be the path travelled by the electric charge of a neuron
or the flow of squares though the oscilloscope at a thousandfold frequency.
could this be consciousness? and what of this fork in the road that lies ahead? i am perhaps simply the agency of the humanity machine
as it sprawls across the planet's surface like slime mold
seeking but oat crumbs
discovering the path of least resistance.
we think ourselves selfish beings but are we perhaps observably a selfless system,
morally colder than we could possibly imagine?


--Side B : kinetic--

these fingers bend back as i press them against the woodgrain surface, stretching skin and sinew.
the humidity of my breath crawls outward along my peach-skin shell when i release the tension from my lungs.
my aching head wretches under the weight of crusting clay.
beneath the vellum skin of rubber gloves, or the ringing of a disturbed spoon on frostbitten ice cream.
i extend and i yield;
to these kinetic sensations.
